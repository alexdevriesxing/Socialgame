# 48-Month Content Roadmap

## Year 1 — Finding a place

Focus: introductions, first friendships, club identity, punctuality and basic school responsibility.

1. Club Recruitment Week
2. Indoor Creativity Fair
3. Inter-Class Games
4. Sakura Festival
5. Midterm Study Marathon
6. Summer Lights Fair
7. Neighborhood Service Day
8. Autumn Showcase
9. Crest Debate Forum
10. Winter Endurance Week
11. Legacy Project Drafts
12. First-Year Gala

## Year 2 — Building influence

Focus: deeper friendships, rivalry, peer mentoring, committee roles and consequences from the first year.

- Student-media election.
- Joint-club project.
- Exchange-student week.
- Regional competitions.
- Friendship misunderstandings.
- Leadership without a title.
- Larger hazards requiring teamwork.
- First major ranking upset.

## Year 3 — Responsibility and pressure

Focus: captaincy, academic direction, mentoring younger students, difficult trade-offs and public expectations.

- Club leadership election.
- Career-shadowing week.
- Regional finals.
- School budget debate.
- Festival crisis.
- Burnout and recovery stories.
- Long-term relationship tests.
- Scholarship and recommendation arcs.

## Year 4 — Legacy

Focus: graduation, succession, final competitions, reconciliation, prom and the meaning of reputation.

- Train successors.
- Resolve unfinished rivalries.
- Build a capstone project.
- Final culture festival.
- Graduation speech selection.
- Prom committee crisis.
- Last monthly ranking.
- Prom King/Queen finale and epilogues.

## Content volume target

A full commercial release should target:

- 20–24 major students.
- 8–12 teachers and staff.
- 4,000+ dialogue lines.
- 250+ class prompts.
- 200+ social events.
- 80+ hazards and school incidents.
- 120+ club activities.
- 48 monthly finales.
- 30+ relationship endings.
- 12+ prom/graduation epilogues.
