# Pixel Art Bible

## Visual target

Commercial-quality 16-bit-inspired anime presentation: crisp silhouettes, compact palettes, readable facial details, warm school interiors and expressive dialogue portraits. The art is original and avoids imitation of any existing franchise.

## Technical rules

- Logical game canvas: 960 × 540.
- School world: 1280 × 800.
- Character frame: 24 × 32 pixels, rendered at 2× in-game.
- Sprite sheet: 3 animation columns × 4 direction rows.
- Portrait cell: 64 × 64 pixels.
- Nearest-neighbor scaling only.
- No blur, antialiasing or subpixel sprite placement.

## Palette language

- Ink/deep navy: outlines, uniforms and UI structure.
- Cream/paper: classrooms, dialogue text and warmth.
- Gold: goals, rank, bells and selected UI.
- Pink/rose: social energy and girls' ladder.
- Sky blue: boys' ladder, information and navigation.
- Mint/green: kindness, energy and safe outcomes.
- Red: lateness, demerits and warnings.

## Character differentiation

Every major student should differ through at least four of the following:

- Hair silhouette and color.
- Fringe shape.
- Accent color.
- Posture and idle motion.
- Portrait expression.
- Club accessory.
- Walk-cycle personality.
- Dialogue-box emblem.

Future production sprites should add club uniforms, formal wear, seasonal outerwear, sports kits and prom outfits while preserving the base silhouette.

## Environment language

The school map uses strong room identities:

- Classrooms: warm cream grid floors and wooden desks.
- Hallway: cool lockers, bulletin board and strong navigation lines.
- Courtyard: green tiles, cherry trees and a central fountain.
- Cafeteria: warm wood, dense tables and a visible serving counter.
- Gym: striped court floor and clear white markings.
- Library: tall shelves and color-coded books.

## Animation roadmap

Vertical slice:

- Three-frame walking in four directions.
- Bell marker pulse.
- UI hover/select states.
- Dialogue type-on effect.
- Confetti prom ending.

Commercial target:

- Six-frame walk/run.
- Two-frame idle personality loops.
- Gesture animations for dialogue scenes.
- Classroom activity animations.
- Weather particles, petals, rain splashes and lighting shifts.
- Character-specific victory, embarrassment and friendship reactions.
