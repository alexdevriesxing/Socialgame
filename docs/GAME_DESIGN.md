# Game Design Document — Sakura Crest: Social Summit

## High concept

A four-year 2D anime school-life simulation where the player builds a reputation through attendance, friendships, clubs, competitions, accomplishments, courage, kindness and reliability. Boys and girls compete on separate monthly ladders. The ultimate public goal is to become Prom King or Prom Queen, but the deeper goal is to graduate with a meaningful network and a school legacy.

## Design pillars

1. **A living timetable** — school life is spatial and time-bound. Classes happen in actual rooms and teachers react to punctuality.
2. **Reputation through conduct** — popularity alone cannot carry the player. Reliability, kindness and visible contributions matter.
3. **Consequences without cruelty** — conflict is family-friendly and resolved through dialogue, humor, planning, mediation or seeking help.
4. **Readable social strategy** — choices clearly shape relationships, stats, accomplishments and monthly score.
5. **Anime warmth, 16-bit discipline** — expressive portraits and dialogue sit on top of crisp, coherent pixel-art environments.

## Core daily loop

1. Arrive before the first bell.
2. Navigate to each scheduled classroom.
3. Complete class dialogue challenges and small activities.
4. Use break, lunch and free period to socialize, investigate events, practice or help others.
5. Attend a selected club.
6. Handle an event, hazard, rumor or non-violent bully situation.
7. Finish daily missions.
8. Receive an end-of-day summary and current rank.

A standard day runs from 07:50 to 15:00. The vertical slice uses accelerated game time so a day lasts a few real-world minutes.

## Long loop

- 20 school days form a simulated month.
- Every month finalizes separate boys' and girls' rankings.
- 12 months form one academic year.
- 4 academic years lead to prom and graduation.
- Monthly themes alter which accomplishments are most valuable.

## Ranking model

The visible standing combines:

- Class and mission accomplishments.
- Club and competition contributions.
- Strength and quality of friendships.
- Charisma, intellect, fitness, talent, kindness, courage and reliability.
- Lateness, absence, demerits, detention and stress penalties.
- Current-month momentum.

The player never directly purchases rank. Rank emerges from behavior.

## Timetable and discipline

Every required period has:

- Start and end time.
- Required room.
- Named teacher.
- Grace period.
- Attendance state: on time, late or absent.

Late arrival triggers a teacher conversation. Response options include honest apology, calm explanation or pushing back. Repeated lateness adds demerits and detention. Missing a class applies a larger reliability and score penalty.

## Social systems

Each recurring NPC has:

- Separate boys' or girls' ladder placement.
- Clique or school-circle affiliation.
- Distinct strengths and weaknesses.
- Relationship score with the player.
- Daily dialogue and longer trust arcs.

Friendships contribute to rank only when they become meaningful. The design discourages shallow interaction spam.

## School circles

- **Skybound Athletics** — fitness, teamwork and school spirit.
- **Velvet Bloom Arts** — performance, visual design and emotional insight.
- **Byte Brigade** — technology, robotics and strategy competitions.
- **Crest Council** — leadership, mediation and event organization.
- **Hallway Legends** — an informal social group built around humor and influence.

These are non-violent school groups, never criminal gangs.

## Family-friendly conflict

“Fights” are represented as social confrontations, debates, competitions, sports rivalries, rumor conflicts and boundary-setting scenes. Valid resolutions include:

- Calling out rules calmly.
- Defusing tension with humor.
- Asking the targeted student what support they want.
- Finding a teacher or peer mediator.
- Creating a practical alternative.
- Walking away without amplifying a rumor.

## Events and hazards

The vertical slice includes:

- Fire drill with a nervous first-year.
- Rainstorm and roof leak.
- Ten-minute power outage.
- Missing phoenix mascot head.
- Hallway rumor spiral.
- Lunch-line intimidation.
- Embarrassing nickname incident.

Future content can add earthquakes drills, heat waves, snow days, festival accidents, sports injuries, broken equipment and transit delays while keeping outcomes safe and constructive.

## Prom ending

The crown is determined from long-term performance, not one final popularity vote. Average monthly placement, conduct and accumulated relationships all matter. A non-crown ending still acknowledges the player's friendships and legacy.
